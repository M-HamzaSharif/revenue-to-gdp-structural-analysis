# A rudimentary Analysis of how revenue-to-GDP behaved after the 18th Amendment of the Constitution (Pakistan)

## Objective
Evaluating whether Pakistan's revenue-to-GDP structurally changed after the 18th Amendment. 

### Methodology
(1) Collection of revenue-to-GDP ratio Pre-2010 and Post-2010 Revenues from Annual Economic Yearbooks (Actual). 
(2) Linear Regression Model trained upon Time-Revenue-to-GDP trend from 1991 to 2009.
(3) Counterfactual projection from 2011 to 2024 using Linear Regression Model.

#### Deductions
(1) Pre-2009, from actual data we can observe that the trend was down-ward. Slope was around -0.25% per year.
(2) Given pre-2009 momentum, it visually seemed the revenue would continue to fall.
(3) The assumption was confirmed by Linear Regression prediction made on the basis of 1991-2009 data.
(4) However, actual data showed that after the 18th amendment the revenue-to-GDP ratio flatenned out a bit; with cyclic downward spikes every 5-7 years (Attributed to latent factors that were not considered for this study)

##### Limitations and Recommendations
(1) The model does not consider any other factors macro-control variables, governance and political breakdowns.
(2) The model trains on a very small range of data; more data and consideration of multiple macro-variables shall allow for better understanding. 
(3) Not a causal inference.
